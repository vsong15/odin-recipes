Description

Donkatsu is a popular Korean dish consisting of breaded and fried pork cutlets, resulting in a crispy exterior and tender meat inside.

Ingredients

  • Pork cutlet - 2 pieces
  • Egg - 2
  • All-purpose flour - 1 cup
  • Panko breadcrumbs - 1 cup
  • Salt - 1 teaspoon
  • Black pepper - 1/2 teaspoon
  • Vegetable oil - for frying

Steps

  1. Tenderize the pork cutlets by pounding them with a meat mallet until they are about 1/2 inch thick.
  2. In a shallow dish, beat the eggs and season with salt and black pepper.
  3. Place the flour and panko breadcrumbs in separate shallow dishes.
  4. Dredge each pork cutlet in flour, then dip it into the beaten eggs, and finally coat it with panko breadcrumbs, pressing gently to adhere.
  5. Heat vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  6. Working in batches, carefully place the breaded pork cutlets in the hot oil and fry until golden brown and cooked through, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  7. Transfer the cooked cutlets to a paper towel-lined plate to drain excess oil.
  8. Serve the donkatsu with steamed rice, shredded cabbage, and tonkatsu sauce.
